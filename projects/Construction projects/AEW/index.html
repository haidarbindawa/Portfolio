ANTICIPATED EARLY WORKS (AEW) FOR NLNG TRAIN 7 PROJECT

Back to Projects

Project Overview

The Anticipated Early Works (AEW) package formed a critical preliminary phase of the NLNG Train 7 expansion project on Bonny Island, Nigeria. The AEW scope was executed to enable early site readiness and de-risk the main EPC works by preparing key development areas ahead of full project mobilization.

As part of the AEW scope, site preparation works for the Temporary Camp Facility (TCF) were carried out across Parcels 1, 2, 3, and 4B/5. The objective was to deliver stable, well-graded, and construction-ready platforms to support early accommodation and infrastructure required for the Train 7 workforce.

The works included surveying and establishment of boundary control points, site clearing and topsoil removal, sand filling, grading, and compaction to achieve required platform elevations. Final surface preparation was completed using structural fill materials, alongside construction of trapezoidal drainage channels and shaping of access routes to ensure proper surface water management and safe site access.

Execution of the AEW activities required close coordination with the main contractor and client representatives, continuous progress monitoring, and strict compliance with NLNG quality, safety, and environmental standards. Completion of the AEW scope enabled timely commencement of follow-on construction works and contributed to overall schedule certainty for the Train 7 programme.

Scope of Work

  • Surveying and establishment of work boundary control points.
  • Site clearing, topsoil removal, and proper disposal/stockpiling of unsuitable material.
  • Sand filling, grading, and compaction to achieve required platform levels.
  • Placement of structural fill as final surface preparation layer.
  • Construction of road-side trapezoidal drainage sections and access route shaping.

My Role & Key Contributions

  • Monitored daily progress and prepared structured daily and weekly reporting.
  • Prepared construction documentation including Method Statements and support documents for work approvals.
  • Developed valuation support records for interim progress claims.
  • Participated in coordination meetings with the main contractor and client representatives.
  • Provided supervisory oversight on selected site activities to ensure safe and compliant execution.

Outcome

The TCF areas were successfully prepared to required elevations and compaction standards, enabling follow-on construction works to proceed without delay. The works ensured stable ground conditions, access, and operational readiness for subsequent facility development under the NLNG Train 7 program.
