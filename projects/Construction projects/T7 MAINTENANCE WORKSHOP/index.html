Project Overview

The T7 Maintenance Workshop project forms part of the NLNG Train 7 development program and includes the civil, structural, and MEP works for a new maintenance facility within the NLNG Industrial Area. The goal is to deliver a durable and functional structure to support long-term maintenance and operations within the plant.

A separate Soil Replacement Scope was executed as an enabling phase prior to structural construction. Geotechnical investigations identified weak organic soil layers that could induce settlement. These were completely excavated and replaced with compacted sandy material from competent strata. The soil replacement was completed successfully, verified through Plate Load Tests (PLT) to confirm the required soil bearing capacity before foundation works commenced.

The ongoing scope covers reinforced-concrete foundations, pedestals, and slab-on-grade construction, as well as plumbing systems, GRVE (Glass Reinforced Vinyl Ester) piping installations, and electrical earthing of foundations to ensure structural and operational integrity. All activities are carried out in accordance with approved drawings, specifications, and NLNG project standards.

My Role & Key Contributions

  • Managed daily site operations and coordinated multidisciplinary teams covering civil, structural, and MEP activities.
  • Supervised and reviewed the soil replacement program, including witnessing and validating Plate Load Tests to confirm soil bearing capacity.
  • Coordinated plumbing installations, GRVE piping, and foundation earthing works, ensuring technical compliance and design integrity.
  • Led client and stakeholder interface, resolving FTQs, facilitating joint inspections, and maintaining project communication.
  • Prepared all construction documentation, including Method Statements, Lifting Plans, Night Work Plans, and other supporting procedures.
  • Reviewed Inspection and Test Plans (ITPs) and other quality control documents to ensure alignment with project specifications and standards.
  • Oversaw project scheduling and progress tracking, developed recovery strategies for observed delays, and ensured milestone achievement.
  • Managed procurement, material verification, and resource planning to meet construction demands.
  • Monitored safety performance and quality assurance throughout all phases of work.
  • Prepared and supported variation claims and progress documentation for management and client approval.
  • Currently coordinating completion of as-built documentation and final project close-out activities.

Outcome

The soil replacement works were completed successfully, providing a verified and stable foundation platform for the maintenance workshop. Structural and MEP installations are substantially complete, with only the grade-slab and documentation close-out remaining. The project demonstrates effective technical coordination, proactive schedule management, and adherence to high standards of quality and safety.
