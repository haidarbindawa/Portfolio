NEW WORKERS’ VILLAGE – FOUNDATION & CIVIL WORKS (NLNG TRAIN 7)

Back to Projects

Project Overview

This project forms part of the NLNG Train 7 development program and involves constructing the complete foundation systems and civil works for the New Workers’ Village in Bonny Island. The village will support accommodation blocks, kitchen/restaurant, water and sewage treatment plants, clinic, ICT centre, laundry, and various recreational facilities.

Scope of Work

  • Land reclamation and site shaping
  • Site levelling and compaction to required platform elevations
  • Construction of drainage networks and stormwater channels
  • Formation of access roads and internal circulation routes
  • Perimeter fencing and security demarcation
  • Reinforced-concrete foundation works (rebar fixing, formwork, concreting)
  • Quality control and HSE supervision throughout execution

My Role & Key Contributions

  • Supported overall project coordination between the contractor, client representatives, and internal teams.
  • Reviewed construction documents, method statements, and quality procedures to ensure alignment with project standards.
  • Monitored project schedules and assessed progress to ensure alignment with planned timelines.
  • Prepared and issued daily, weekly, and monthly reports covering progress, materials, manpower, and key site activities.
  • Attended and documented weekly coordination and progress meetings with the client and stakeholders.
  • Tracked action items and followed up with site teams to ensure timely closure.
  • Measured works completed and prepared valuation documents for monthly payment certification.
  • Collaborated with the commercial team to verify quantities and support approval of interim valuations.
  • Performed periodic site visits to review work quality, check compliance with drawings/specifications, and raise any technical concerns for resolution.
  • Assisted with technical documentation, inspection records, and quality reporting.

Outcome

The foundation and enabling civil works were completed to specification and handed over as verified stable platform areas ready for follow-on structural and architectural works. Quality checks and HSE processes were maintained throughout, with punch lists and inspection records issued for client approval. The delivery ensured minimal schedule slippage and provided a robust base for the camp facilities to be constructed.
