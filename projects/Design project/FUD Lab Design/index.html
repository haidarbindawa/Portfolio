Project Overview

This project involved the structural design of a new Physics Laboratory building for Federal University Dutse. The assignment covered preliminary studies, geotechnical verification, structural modeling, foundation design, and preparation of coordinated construction documents to support safe and efficient project delivery.

Scope of Work

  • Preliminary investigation and site data review
  • Geotechnical verification and interpretation of soil test results
  • Coordination with architectural and MEP teams
  • Structural analysis and load determination
  • Design of beams, slabs, columns, staircases, and roof system
  • Foundation system design based on soil parameters
  • Preparation of structural drawings and calculation reports
  • Review and quality checks for constructability and compliance

My Role & Key Contributions

  • Performed structural analysis and modelling
  • Verified geotechnical inputs for foundation selection and sizing
  • Designed all structural members and ensured code compliance
  • Reviewed architectural integration and structural layouts
  • Produced detailed structural drawings and technical reports

Outcome

The structural design was successfully completed and delivered with full documentation, providing the university with a safe, durable, and cost-efficient building solution tailored to laboratory functionality. The final drawings and calculations offered clear guidance for construction, minimized potential site challenges, and ensured that the building could be executed without delays arising from design ambiguities.
